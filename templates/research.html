Our Research References

The research that informed ThyroPredict's development

Prevalence and Risk Factors of Subclinical Thyroid Disease

Citation: Kim, Y.A. & Park, Y.J. (2014). Endocrinology and Metabolism, 29(1), 20-29. https://doi.org/10.3803/EnM.2014.29.1.20

Kim and Park (2014) provide one of the most widely referenced analyses of subclinical thyroid dysfunction. Their study highlights how subtle hormonal imbalances—especially slight shifts in TSH and Free T4—can signal early thyroid disruption even before symptoms appear.

How to Interpret Thyroid Function Tests

Citation: Koulouri, O., & Gurnell, M. (2013). Clinical Medicine (London), 13(3), 282-286. https://doi.org/10.7861/clinmedicine.13-3-282

Koulouri & Gurnell (2013) provide one of the most practical and clinician-focused explanations of how to interpret TSH, Free T4, and Free T3 results. Their work highlights the importance of evaluating thyroid hormones in context—considering pituitary function, illness, medication effects, and the dynamic relationship between TSH and circulating thyroid hormones.

Risk Estimation and Prediction Using Machine-Learning Methods

Citation: Kruppa, J., Ziegler, A., & König, I. R. (2012). Human Genetics, 131(10), 1639-1654. https://doi.org/10.1007/s00439-012-1194-y

This paper explores how machine-learning methods can enhance risk estimation and patient classification beyond traditional statistical techniques. The authors show that while genetic association studies identify susceptibility variants, translating those into accurate individual risk predictions is challenging. Their review emphasises that for model-building (especially in complex diseases) features like calibration, discrimination, and validation on independent data are vital.
